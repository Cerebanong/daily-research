# Weekly Databricks Briefing — Week of 2026-03-24 to 2026-03-30

**To:** kevinlfrank@gmail.com

This week marked Databricks' boldest strategic move yet — entering the security market with an agentic SIEM — while the platform continued its rapid feature expansion across Unity Catalog, serverless compute, and AI-powered data engineering.

## Top Stories

### 1. Databricks Enters Security Market with Lakewatch Agentic SIEM

Databricks made its biggest strategic bet of 2026 by launching Lakewatch, an open agentic SIEM designed to combat increasingly sophisticated AI-powered attacks. The launch was accompanied by the acquisitions of Antimatter (data security) and SiftD.ai (threat intelligence), signaling a full-stack security play built on the lakehouse. Lakewatch leverages Databricks' existing strengths in data processing and AI to create a security platform that can autonomously detect, investigate, and respond to threats.

**Source:** [Databricks Newsroom](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-enters-security-market-launch-lakewatch-new-open-agentic) — Published 2026-03-24
**Why it matters:** This positions Databricks as a direct competitor to Splunk and Microsoft Sentinel, leveraging the cost advantages of lakehouse architecture for security analytics.

### 2. Azure Databricks Lakebase Reaches General Availability

Lakebase, Databricks' managed serverless Postgres service designed for AI agents, reached general availability across 14 Azure regions. Adoption has grown at more than twice the rate of Databricks' data warehousing product, suggesting strong market demand for agent-native database infrastructure. The service provides a familiar Postgres interface while integrating with the broader Databricks ecosystem.

**Source:** [Databricks Blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/whats-new-azure-databricks-fabcon-2026-lakebase-lakeflow-and-genie) — Published 2026-03-25
**Why it matters:** Lakebase's rapid adoption validates the demand for agent-optimized databases and strengthens Databricks' position in the emerging agentic AI infrastructure stack.

### 3. Genie Code Launches, Bringing Agentic Engineering to Data Work

Databricks launched Genie Code, which brings agentic engineering capabilities to everyday data work. The tool allows users to interact with Unity Catalog tables using natural language, generating code-based answers and automating data engineering tasks. Combined with the Sample Data Explorer reaching GA, this represents a significant step toward making data engineering accessible to business users through AI.

**Source:** [Databricks Newsroom](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases) — Published 2026-03-11
**Why it matters:** Genie Code continues Databricks' strategy of embedding AI agents throughout the platform, reducing the barrier between business questions and data answers.

### 4. Unity Catalog Gets Governed Tags (GA) and Discover Experience (Beta)

Two significant Unity Catalog enhancements arrived this week. Governed Tags reached general availability, allowing administrators to define controlled tag sets for consistent metadata governance across the lakehouse. Separately, the new Discover experience launched in beta, embedding business context, trust signals, and access controls directly into the data discovery workflow. Health indicators for tables are now visible on schema and table overview pages.

**Source:** [Databricks Release Notes](https://docs.databricks.com/aws/en/release-notes/product/2026/march) — Published 2026-03-28
**Why it matters:** These updates strengthen Unity Catalog's position as a comprehensive governance layer, moving beyond access control to embed trust and business context into every data interaction.

### 5. AI Runtime Brings GPU Support to Serverless Compute

Databricks released AI Runtime, bringing GPU support to serverless compute for the first time. Designed for deep learning workloads, AI Runtime eliminates the need for dedicated GPU clusters, allowing data teams to run ML training and inference on-demand. This was accompanied by the 5X-Large SQL warehouse size reaching beta, providing additional compute headroom for large-scale analytical workloads.

**Source:** [Databricks Release Notes](https://docs.databricks.com/aws/en/release-notes/product/2026/march) — Published 2026-03-25
**Why it matters:** Serverless GPU compute removes a major friction point for ML teams and lowers the barrier to running deep learning workloads on the Databricks platform.
