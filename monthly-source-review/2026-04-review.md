## 4. Search Query Refinements

- Data Governance queries are pulling from security/forensics sources (Digital Forensics Magazine, SharkStriker) that are tangential to core data governance.

### Recommended Query Changes

| Category | Refinement |
|---|---|
| **AI Innovation** | Add source-specific filters or site: operators for newly added publications. Include queries like "AI model release," "AI breakthrough," "AI product launch" to target innovation-specific content rather than general AI news. |
| **AI Governance** | Add queries targeting "AI regulation 2026," "AI Act implementation," "AI policy framework," "state AI law." Include site-specific queries for IAPP and Atlantic Council. |
| **Data Governance** | Tighten queries to exclude cybersecurity/forensics noise. Use terms like "data governance framework," "data quality management," "CDO data strategy" rather than broad "data governance" which pulls security-adjacent results. |
| **Databricks** | Current queries are working well. Consider adding "Databricks partner" or "Databricks customer" queries to catch ecosystem news from third-party sources. |

- **Recommendation:** Before removing any source, verify against at least 4 weeks of continuous collection data. The ratings above should be treated as directional indicators, not definitive judgments.
- The apparent 0% hit rate for AI Innovation and AI Governance configured sources is a strong enough signal to warrant immediate investigation, even with limited data.
